PPG Place

PPG Place, designed by architect Philip Johnson, is an iconic complex at the heart of Pittsburgh. Comprising six diamond-shaped glass towers connected by a central atrium, the site was completed in 1984. The main 40-story tower houses the headquarters of PPG Industries. The bold use of glass and marble gives the complex a modern and elegant aesthetic, and the Wintergarden, a glass structure, adds a distinctive public dimension to the whole. PPG Place is a noteworthy example of postmodern architecture, blending contemporary and traditional elements in a unique manner.
