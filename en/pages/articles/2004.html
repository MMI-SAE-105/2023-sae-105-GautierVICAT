MoMA Expension

The major renovation of the MoMA in 2004 aimed to modernize the facilities and expand exhibition space to enhance the visitor experience. While architect Philip Johnson played a significant role in the initial design of the MoMA in 1939, the 2004 renovation was led by other architects with the goal of meeting the contemporary needs of the museum. Therefore, although Johnson's legacy is ingrained in the architectural history of the MoMA, he was not directly involved in the specific renovations of 2004.
