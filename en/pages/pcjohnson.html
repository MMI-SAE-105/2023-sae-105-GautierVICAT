Philip C. Johnson

Picture of Philip Johnson

"All architecture is shelter, but great architecture is the design of a space that contains, embraces, enhances, or stimulates the people within that space."
Philip C. Johnson

Philip Johnson (1906-2005) He was a renowned American architect, influential in the development of modern architecture.

His most famous works

He co-organized the "International Style" exhibition in 1932, and later designed the Glass House in 1949, an iconic residence characterized by entirely glass walls. His career also included major projects such as the Seagram Building in New York. Johnson played a key role at MoMA and was criticized for his controversial political views. He passed away in 2005, leaving behind a lasting legacy in the history of modern architecture.
